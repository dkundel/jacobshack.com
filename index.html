AboutjacobsHack!

"I Hack, Therefore I Am!". Under this slogan the foundation for jacobsHack! was laid when on Feb 1, 2014 the students of Jacobs University met to code for 19 hours on whatever project they considered interesting. On October 4th, 2014 we then took the next step and opened jacobsHack! for external students. In total 85 students from 30 nations participated in jacobsHack! Fall creating one of the most culturally diverse hackathons in Europe.

This year we are returning aiming to create one of the greatest and most diverse hackathons in Europe. Bigger and more exiting then before while sticking to our initial motto:

I Hack, Therefore I Am!

What is jacobsHack!?

24h of hacking

You have this project you always wanted to code but you never found the time for? Now you have the chance to dedicate 24 hours on this project. The only condition is:  You will only be judged on what you wrote within these 24 hours

Meet fellow hackers!

You have the opportunity to meet fellow programmers from all over the world, exchange your knowledge or even find your future business partner with whom you will found your first start-up.

Prizes and Freebies

Thanks to our sponsors you will have the chance to win a variety of awesome prizes and even if you don't win we can promise you you won't leave empty handed!

Career Opportunities

Get the opportunity to meet some of the engineers and recruiters of our sponsors, make contacts and get the chance to potentially interview with some of them.

Where Does jacobsHack! Take Place?

jacobsHack! takes place on the campus of the international university  Jacobs University Bremen  in Bremen, Germany. The campus is home for over 700 Bachelor students and in total over 1200 students representing 114 nations creating a truly diverse experience that we will try to deliver to the participants of jacobsHack!

How to apply?

Simply head to our  Registration Portal  and register there. Convince us why you are amazing and we will hopefully see you in Bremen in October! We'll accept applicants on a rolling basis.

What is the application process?

We are looking for a diverse group of hackers at our hackathon. For this we will ask you a couple of questions in the application. That said we are looking for anyone who is creative or just simply passionate for technology. You don't necessarily have to have any previous hackathon experience. We will ask a variety of questions that will give you the chance to show us why you are awesome!

I'm not a student anymore. Can I apply?

This hackathon is specificially designed for university students. Unfortunately we are not able to accept anyone who is not currently enrolled at a university or graduated longer than one year ago.

What do I need to bring?

Aside of your laptop, phone, chargers and special hardware that you might need for your hack you should bring the following thinks:

Enthusiasm. Basic toiletries and a sleeping bag in case you are planning to sleep might be useful as well! Food and drinks will be provided during the event. Please make sure to mention special dietary requirements in your registration.

What should I hack on?

Anything that you are passionate about. No worries you don't have to come with an idea or a team fixed. You can meet great people at the event and probably together you can come up with a great idea.

jacobsHack! does not have an overall theme meaning you can qualify with any project that you code within the 24 hours to win the main prize. However there will be some separate prizes that will be themed. These themes will be announced at the event.

What can I win?

We will have a variety of prizes to win. From a main prize that everyone qualifies for, who codes something in the 24 hours and presents it, to themed prizes provided by our sponsors. Some prizes might be cash prizes while others might be products or vouchers.

How do I get to jacobsHack!?

jacobsHack! will take place at the Jacobs University Bremen in the north of Bremen, Germany. Bremen has its own airport with Ryanair flights from all over Europe and the cities Hamburg and Hannover are very close with frequent train and bus connections. Once you arrive in Bremen you can follow the instructions here: How to get to Jacobs University Bremen

Will there be travel reimbursement?

We are planning to provide students with travel reimbursement but we do not have concrete numbers yet how much we can offer. We will provide more information once we started accepting applicants.

I'm arriving a day before the event. Can you host me?

We can't provide you with a proper room but closer to the event we will get in touch with everyone to estimate how many people are arriving earlier and we'll give our best to find some fellow students at the university that might be able to host you for one night in their rooms.

Who organizes jacobsHack!?

jacobsHack! was founded and is organized by students and alumni of the Jacobs University Bremen. You can see the whole team and their contacts in our  Team section.
